TEST RUN — esp_now_tripwire (peer-to-peer RSSI tripwire)
Date: 2026-04-19
Boards:
  - Beacon (ESP-32 #1)    flashed with esp_now_beacon sketch
  - Receiver (ESP-32 #2)  flashed with esp_now_tripwire sketch
                          sensor_id = tripwire_receiver
Server: http://10.0.0.113:5000/motion

----------------------------------------------------------
ATTEMPT 1 — boards placed close together (~1-2 feet)
----------------------------------------------------------
BASELINE: ~-27 dBm   (very strong, near radio saturation)
NORMAL RSSI: ~-24 dBm
BEHAVIOR: frequent false triggers every few seconds even with
  nobody near the beam. Random dropouts to -31/-32/-36 dBm were
  firing the delta>=4 dBm threshold.

SAMPLE OUTPUT:
  RSSI=-24 dBm  baseline=-27.0  delta=3
  RSSI=-31 dBm  baseline=-27.1  delta=4   (false trigger)
  RSSI=-36 dBm  baseline=-27.0  delta=10  (false trigger)

DIAGNOSIS:
At -24 dBm the radio is saturated. Tiny environmental events
(WiFi interference, multipath, packet retries) cause outsized
RSSI swings that pass the threshold.

----------------------------------------------------------
ATTEMPT 2 — boards separated across the room
----------------------------------------------------------
BASELINE: settled in the -50 to -70 dBm range (typical motion-
  sensing sweet spot)
BEHAVIOR: no false triggers. Walking through the direct line
  between the two boards reliably produced one "tripwire_broken"
  alert per pass. Adaptive EMA baseline absorbed small long-term
  drifts without causing extra alerts.
RESULT: PASS

----------------------------------------------------------
KEY TAKEAWAYS (for the slideshow)
----------------------------------------------------------
1. Peer-to-peer ESP-NOW measures the actual path between the
   sensor nodes (not the router), which is a cleaner, more
   defensible definition of "motion in a specific line."
2. Adaptive (EMA) baseline absorbs slow environmental drift —
   no more constant re-tripping when someone sits in the beam.
3. RSSI has a working range. Boards must not be too close or
   the radio saturates and random jitter creates false alarms.
